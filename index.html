FAQ

Common questions about visa tracking

What is the Schengen 90/180 rule? +

The Schengen 90/180 rule means you can stay in the Schengen Area for a maximum of 90 days within any rolling 180-day window. It's not a fixed 6-month period — it moves every day. Overstaying can result in a 2-year re-entry ban and a permanent record in the EU's Entry/Exit System (EES).

What happens if I overstay my visa in Europe? +

Overstaying a Schengen visa can result in a re-entry ban of up to 2 years, fines, deportation, and a flag in the EU Entry/Exit System. The EES makes overstays automatically detectable at every Schengen border — there's nowhere to hide. One overstay can shut you out of 27 European countries.

How do I track my Schengen days automatically? +

Travisaro tracks your Schengen days automatically. Add your entry and exit dates for each trip, and Travisaro calculates your 90/180-day usage in real time — and shows exactly how many days you have left. You can also validate future trips before booking to confirm you won't overstay.

Is there an app to track visa expiry dates? +

Yes — Travisaro tracks all your visa expiry dates in one place. It sends automated alerts 30, 14, and 7 days before any visa expires. The AI Smart Scan feature lets you photograph any visa stamp or sticker and extract the details automatically, so you don't have to type anything manually.

Can I track multiple passports and visas? +

Yes. Travisaro's paid plans (Traveler and Pro) support multiple passports. If you hold dual citizenship, you can add each passport separately and track all your visas across both. The app keeps your travel history separate per passport and calculates remaining days for each one independently.

Does Travisaro track tax residency days? +

Yes. Travisaro's Tax Days feature tracks how many days you've spent in each country this calendar year and shows it against the 183-day tax residency threshold. Cards turn green (safe), amber (watch out), orange (getting close), or red (183+ days — you may be a tax resident). This helps digital nomads avoid accidentally triggering tax residency in a country.
